A new category on the spectrum?

We support this change as it allows us to specialize on this often overlooked and highly misunderstood, yet significant population of Individuals with autism. Narrowing the spectrum in such a way enables us to focus directly on this group with much greater precision in a manner suited to their specific needs.

In recent years, there’s been more demand for housing options for highly autistic, nonverbal individuals. As more people get diagnosed, the need for specialized homes has increased. New types of housing, like group homes, supported apartments, and intentional communities, are being created to help meet these needs.

But it’s not all smooth sailing—there are still challenges. Funding and policies make it tough to build enough of these homes, and there isn’t enough data to really understand how much is needed. While progress is being made, there’s still a lot of work to do to make sure everyone has access to the right kind of housing.

A separate category of profound autism is warranted because the needs of these children may be neglected if they are not identified.
