How we engage is as important to our mission as the lessons themselves. We recognize that each individual may have different learning abilities, they may assimilate at a different pace and have many different limitations.

Anxiety and its negative effects seem to always be present in most non-verbal or minimally verbal people. These effects seem amplified when they cannot articulate feelings desires opinions or even the simplest of communications.

This is often the result of their inability to conjure the words that express those wishes desires ideas thoughts and hopes. These individuals can become more frustrated by the need to articulate justifications for things they feel are right, leading to even more anxiety. They are 100% reliant on those who are charged with their care and their ability to decipher what the non verbal individuals are trying so desperately to convey.

Sometimes they must resort to extreme behaviors in hopes of getting enough attention to try to make it known that they are not being understood. This can lead to further anxiety as well as negative consequences like misunderstanding punishment or rejection.

The Leg Up Program: Empowers Adults with other Disabilities to help Mentor Non-Verbal Adults in pursuit of Expressive Language Development

In a world that often underestimates individuals with disabilities, the "Leg Up Program" is a unique and highly supportive mentoring plan administered under the watchful eye of trained caregivers.

It is designed to foster a profound sense of mutual empowerment. This pilot program pairs adults with disabilities—those whose impairments are not related to language development—with non-verbal adults who are seeking to enhance their expressive language skills.

The supervised pairs engage in a fun shared experience together and then use that experience to associate vocabulary words and ideas about it., giving them the opportunity to communicate about something they share in common.

The essence of the Leg Up mentorship incorporates contextual learning, where both participants gain valuable life experience. The mentor gains strength, dignity, and purpose through their collaboration while the mentee becomes immersed in a culture of communication revolving around fun and camaraderie.
