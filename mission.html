Our Mission

Our mission is to provide a supportive community for autistic young adults who experience communication challenges. We believe in the power of connection and inclusivity, providing a safe space where everyone can thrive.

Core Principles

  • All people deserve to be understood and supported
  • Disability and challenges do not have to prevent a meaningful life
  • Everyone deserves a solid chance in life no matter what obstacles they face
  • The world is enhanced by neurodiversity and non-verbal autistic people teach us to learn new ways of communicating.
  • Safety is the first consideration, and all development follows from there

Our Aim

We aim to engage with, teach, and learn from a community of language-challenged adults and developmentally disabled individuals from all walks of life. Our strategy is to build a certified autism-friendly community where participants may engage with others at their comfort level from daily visits to part- time to full residency.

Our Answer to the Challenge

The challenge that many parents/caregivers face, is the overwhelming prospect that there is no place for their aging family members to reside and thrive once they can no longer provide for them. This issue is growing rapidly and creates enormous emotional strain for parents.

Statistics

Autism is one of the fastest-growing disabilities. Non-verbal and minimally verbal individuals make up 25-30% of the autistic population.

Our Community

The center provides a day camp experience with a variety of therapeutic activities and outdoor experiences. The hub of the center includes a kitchen, sensory spaces, individual living spaces or tiny home living spaces and the potential to build farm to table, store and a restaurant, and an event center. The possibilities are limitless when the barriers to untapping the potential within the members of this community.

What We Teach

We offer life skills training, from basic personal hygiene to advanced community participation. We emphasize mentorship through our “leg up” program, pairing advanced participants with beginners.
