Our Mission

Share on Facebook Share on Twitter Share on LinkedIn

Our mission is to provide a supportive community for autistic young adults who experience communication challenges. We believe in the power of connection and inclusivity, providing a safe space where everyone can thrive.

Core Principles

  • Behavior is an important form of communication.
  • Disability and challenges do not have to prevent a meaningful life.
  • The world is enhanced by neurodiversity and non-verbal autistic people who inspire us to learn new ways of communicating.
  • Helping others is the most rewarding of all human experiences.
  • Safety is the first consideration, and all development follows from there.

Our Vision

Our primary focus is our commitment to provide support and to provide tailored challenges commensurate with each individual’s skill levels abilities and sensitivities to as many/severely autistic individuals as possible. We will offer both day camps and residential communities. By engaging with, teaching, and learning from a community of language-challenged autistic adults and developmentally disabled individuals, we seek to: 1. Provide severely autistic individuals the means to discover and develop their talents and help them build a meaningful enjoyable life. 2. Develop best practices and create a standard model for supporting severely autistic individuals that any organization can adopt partially or wholly.

Challenge

The challenge that Many parents/caregivers face, is the overwhelming prospect that there is no place for their aging family members to reside and thrive once they can no longer provide for them. This issue is growing rapidly and creates enormous emotional strain for parents.

Statistics

Autism is among the fastest-growing developmental disabilities in the world. Of the autistic population, an estimated 25-30% are non-verbal or minimally verbal, highlighting the critical need for tailored programs and resources to support their unique communication and learning challenges.

Our Community

The center provides a day camp experience with a variety of therapeutic activities and outdoor experiences. Our “Hub” includes a kitchen, sensory spaces, individual living spaces or tiny home living spaces and the potential to build farm to table, store and restaurant, and an event center. We aim/strive to eliminate barriers that stifle potential for each participant. The possibilities are limitless when eliminating these the barriesrs to untapping the potential within each member of this community.

What We Teach

We offer life skills training, from basic personal hygiene to advanced community participation. We emphasize mentorship through our leg up program, pairing advanced participants with beginners.
